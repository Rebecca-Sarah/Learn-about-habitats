The Rainforest

The ecosystem in the Rainforest is a vital environment to protect as it may only take up 2% of the Earth’s surface area however it provides nearly 50% of the flora and fauna with habitat. It plays a vital role on Earth as close to 20% of the World’s oxygen is from the Rainforest and can be known as the ‘lungs of the planet’. Additionally, it is key to regulating the climate conditions of the planet as the ecosystems help to protect against different natural disasters such as floods, erosions and droughts. There are many reasons why the Rainforest has been so sought over and destroyed as there are many different wants for human use and consumption that are found there, for example, coffee, cocoa powder, vanilla, timber and black pepper as well as different sources of food and medicine. The span of the Rainforest habitat used to accumulate up to six million square miles, however due to deforestation that has decreased down to almost three million.

There are many different types of flora and fauna found in the Rainforest which include orchids, Mountain Gorilla, Blue Morpho Butterfly, Poison Dart Frog, Green Anaconda and many more. There are also tribes still living in the Rainforest where it provides all the needs they need to survive including water, food and shelter.

To help protect this special habitat, there are many steps that can be taken which include donating to different charities actively fighting against the deforestation taking place, planting more trees where there used to be patches of forest, parks could be found and started to protect the surroundings to ensure they are protected and cannot be touched by humans to be destroyed, and lastly, to expand your knowledge on the world we live in and keep up-to-date with different news on what is happening in the Rainforest.

The African Savanna

The African Savannas are grasslands that are flat which are home to many different plants and trees which provide food, shelter and water to the many different species of animals that live there. On the video there are many different animals shown, detailing what their role is in their habitat to help the eco-system thrive. The animals living there have adapted to be able to survive the conditions that they may face which includes having a shortage of water and food. Many of the animals migrate or hibernate until the season has finished and they would be able to find different sources for their needs again. Camouflage is another way animals have adapted to be able to protect themselves from different predators that are roaming the grounds.

The African Savanna can be protected by being respected by the humans visiting it and ensuring they are doing the most to conserve the land and the living things that are there. There are also many different restoration projects that people can volunteer with to help them reach their target of making the Savanna a safe place and ensure the damage that has been done won’t keep affecting the Savanna in the future.

Kelp Forest

Kelp forests are made up of large brown algae that are found in the ocean, this ecosystem is usually found in cold shallow areas of water that are full of nutrients, they start their growth at the bottom of the seafloor and grow until the surface of the water, kelp can look like tall plants, hence where it got the name of a forest. Some of the species growing in a kelp forest can grow up to forty-five metres underwater which makes it the perfect place for shelter for different animals and marine species. There are many different species that use kelp forests for necessities such as shelter, food and protection, fish and other sea live use kelp forests for shelter especially during storms and from predators. Different birds such as seagulls and great blue herons and shore birds often use kelp forests for feeding. Kelp forests also are vital for protecting the shoreline from severe winds and storms as the forests help fight against erosion.

There are many different factors that are threatening the habitat of a kelp forest, one of which includes global warming as the waters are rising in temperature that means that the cold water isn’t bringing in the correct nutrients needed to keep the algae healthy, which leads to the kelp becoming weaker and not surviving. Another threat to life in the kelp forests are destructive fishing practices which can ruin the life that is being supported within the forests under the waterline.

Fortunately, there are different ways we can help the population of kelp forests, which is to bring more attention to what is happening involving different fishing practices and changing methods to ensure the least amount of damage to our surroundings is happening, this should in turn allow the 5% of kelp forests left to have time to recover. Reforestation has been another method to have been used and it seen to be one of the best ways to restore different habitats and lost biodiversity under the water’s surface.

The Arctic

The Arctic is home to many fascinating creatures that live both above and under the sea that have adapted over time to live in such a cold climate. It is made up of glaciers, icebergs, frozen fresh water and ice. The glaciers and the icebergs in the Arctic make up 20% of the Earth’s supply of fresh water. The animals that live in the Arctic include seals, sea lions, walruses, sharks, crabs, baleen whales, orcas and narwhales. The animals that live there are a range of carnivores and herbivores, and get everything they need including shelter, food and water within their ecosystem.

The Arctic Oceans can very easily be under attack as they do not belong to a nation which means there is no protection status around them which makes them open to a range of dangerous activities that can include poaching. It is hoped that a strong Global Ocean Treaty will be presented which will lead to the Arctic Ocean becoming a sanctuary and somewhere safe for the creatures that live within the habitat.
